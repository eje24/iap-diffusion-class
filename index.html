Generative AI with Stochastic Differential Equations - MIT IAP 2025

Diffusion and flow-based models have become the state of the art for generative AI across a wide range of data modalities, including images, videos, shapes, molecules, music, and more! This course aims to build up the mathematical framework underlying these models from first principles. At the end of the class, students will have built a toy image diffusion model from scratch, and along the way, will have gained hands-on experience with the mathematical toolbox of stochastic differential equations that is useful in many other fields. This course is ideal for students who want to develop a principled understanding of the theory and practice of generative AI.

Labs

There will be 3 labs given as homework. The students will code up "MitJourney", a small image diffusion model. The labs will guide you to build up this model from scratch step-by-step. In office hours, we will provide help and guidance to solve the labs.
  • Lab 1: Building and simulating SDE models
  • Lab 2: Training SDE models
  • Lab 3: Building a Conditional Image Generator

Prerequisites: Linear algebra, real analysis, and basic probability theory. Students should be familiar with Python and have some experience with PyTorch.

Questions? Email either Peter or Ezra!

Remark about LLMs: This course does not cover Large Language Models (LLMs). LLMs focus on discrete data such as text, while in this class we focus on data lying in continuous spaces such as images, videos, or protein structures.

6.S183: In the first half of IAP (before the start of this class), there will be another class A Practical Introduction to Diffusion Models. Students who prefer a more application-focused viewpoint should also consider taking that class.
